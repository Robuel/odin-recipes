Nilagang Baboy or Baka (Pork or Beed Stew)

Description

A very classic Filipino dish that I can guarantee you EVERY Filipino knows! It's quite simplistic since it's geenrally just a soup full of meat and vegetables. It gets better as the soup gets older (after a day) since the vegetables have more time to seep into the stew giving you a flavorful and hearty soup!

Ingredients

  • 750 grams of pork belly or beef cuts
  • 1 head of chinese cabbage (washed/cleaned)
  • 4 medium sized sweet potatoes
  • 3 fingers/pcs of sweet plantain or ripe saba/cardava banana
  • 3 to 4 heads of sweet corn broken down into smaller cobs
  • 2 tablespoon or black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of MSG
  • 1 pork bouillon cube
  • 1 large finely diced red onion
  • 2 cloves of finely crushed garlic or garlic paste
  • 3.5 liters of water

Steps

  1. Sweat the onions and garlic on the pressure cooker or pot in medium heat for 3 minutes
  2. Add the pork or beef cuts
  3. Add the black pepper, msg, and fish sauce then mix them for a minute
  4. Add the corn, sweet potato, and plaintain/saba
  5. Add the water and throw in the pork bouillon cube
  6. Let it cook on high heat for an hour and thirty minutes (pressure cooker) or two hours and thirty minutes (normal pot)
  7. Taste the soup and add salt if needed
  8. Once you're satisfied with the saltiness you can then add the chinese cabbage to cook for 2 minutes.
  9. Eat it as is or serve it with rice!

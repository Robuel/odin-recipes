Pork and Red Kidney Beans Stew

Description

This dish was something that my dad always made whenever I would request for it. It's the dish that's responsible for me liking beans which isn't quite common among Filipinos since everyone is addicted to rice. I like eating this dish with rice too, but it can get quite heavy so I prefer eating it as is. Just a bowl full of soft, tender red kidney beans with soft chunks of pork and fat swimming in a thick bean base flavored with paprika and smoked bacon bits.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of pork belly or pork shoulder
  • 750 grams of softened red kidney beans
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika powder
  • 1 tablespoon or black pepper
  • 250 grams of smoked bacon
  • 1 pork bouillon cube
  • 1 large red onion finely diced
  • 2 cloves of finely crushed garlic or garlic paste
  • 3.5 liters of water

Steps

  1. Sweat the onions and garlic on the pressure cooker at medium heat for 3 minutes
  2. Add the red kidney beans
  3. Add the pork and smoked bacon
  4. Add the paprika powder and black pepper
  5. Stir them all together to mix the spices
  6. Add the water and throw in the pork bouillon cube
  7. Let it cook on high heat for an hour (pressure cooker) or two hours (normal pot)
  8. Taste the soup and add salt if needed
  9. Once you're satisfied with the saltiness you can eat it as is or serve it with rice!
